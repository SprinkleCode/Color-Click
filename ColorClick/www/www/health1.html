Health,Wellness and Relationships!


It's thought that only white teen's are affected by eating disorders, but that's just a myth. Anyone can be affected. It's not that teen's of color aren't affected. They're just less likely to seek treatment. Recent studies have shown that eating disorders are on the rise in young women of color. This could be from how pop culture portrays black women or from society telling us how we should look. At the end of the day, you should never feel less than beautiful in your own skin. You don't need anyone telling you what your beautiful is. But if you're struggling, never be afraid to seek treatment. There is always someone who will help you. But what if you are at an unhealthy weight and you're ready to make a change? Exercise and healthy eating are the way to go! 150 minutes of moderate exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous exercise a week can make all the difference in your life. This exercise needs to be accompanied with healthy eating. There are tons of recipes you can find online to help you in your journey. But remember, only go for it if you're ready and want to. Not because someone told you to.

Young teen girls are always trying to lose weight and stress out about it. You don't need a special diet like a low-carb or high-protein diet to lose weight. The best way to lose weight is to get the right mix of nutrients and energy your body needs. This is so you can feel great in your own body. It can be a bit harder for some people to lose weight because of their genes or because of things around them, such as the food choices in their house. But with the right support and a good plan, you can get to a healthy weight. It can be tough to need to lose weight. At times, you may feel frustrated, angry, and other emotions. That's normal. While working to lose weight, remember to congratulate yourself for any successes. Don't be so hard on your body.

Don't let a boy break your heart because your heart is precious. because they come and go.
